About Andressa

Data Analyst & Environmental Engineer

Who is Andressa?

Brazilian, 25 years old, Andressa has data analysis as the central axis of her academic and professional development. During her undergraduate studies, she completed three scientific initiation projects, which provided her first structured experience with data handling, Microsoft Office tools, and statistical procedures. In her mandatory internship and subsequent position at an environmental consultancy, she further advanced her skills in technical reporting, data visualization, and geospatial data analysis.

She later moved to Portugal to pursue a master’s degree at IPB, where she developed her thesis applying advanced methods for data processing, interpretation, and integration within a scientific research framework. After completing her degree, she chose to remain in the country and continued her engagement in two funded research projects — in 2024 and 2025 — while also contributing voluntarily to two additional projects. Across these experiences, she worked extensively with environmental monitoring data, statistical analyses, modelling, and geospatial datasets, building a strong foundation in turning complex information into actionable insights.

Currently residing in Portugal, Andressa continues to strengthen her analytical competencies and has recently expanded her focus to Python programming, aiming to enhance her ability to automate, analyse, and systematize data in scientific, corporate, and applied contexts, preparing for versatile professional challenges in both research and industry environments.

Professional Journey

  • Monitored gardens and agricultural practices, including soil sampling, laboratory analyses, and calculation of water balance and crop water requirements;

  • Prepared technical and scientific reports, extensively applying geospatial analyses to support monitoring and decision-making;
  • Performed environmental risk assessments, prepared impact studies, and contributed to sustainable management practices;
  • Mentored students in experimental design, data collection, and data analysis;
  • Organized and participated in events, workshops, and scientific outreach activities.
